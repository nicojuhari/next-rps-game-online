## Recommended Outline

### H1: How to Play Rock Paper Scissors Online With Friends (No Download, No Account)

**Opening hook (2-3 sentences):** Paint the scenario — you and a friend need to settle something, you're not in the same room, texting back and forth is awkward and unfair (both players see each other's choice before revealing). There is a better way.

### H2: The Problem With Texting Rock Paper Scissors
- One player always sees the other's choice before "revealing"
- No simultaneous reveal = not fair
- Enter: real-time online RPS

### H2: How to Play Rock Paper Scissors Online With Friends — Step by Step
*(This is where we walk through our own product)*

#### H3: Step 1 — Go to the 2-Player Game
- No account, no app download, open in any browser

#### H3: Step 2 — Share the Link With Your Friend
- One click to copy the unique game room URL
- Friend opens on their device — any device, any location

#### H3: Step 3 — Both Players Pick at the Same Time
- Simultaneous reveal eliminates cheating
- Best of 3 rounds for a fair outcome

#### H3: Step 4 — See Who Wins
- Instant result, no waiting, no manual score-tracking

### H2: Why Simultaneous Reveal Matters (The Fair Game Principle)
- Explains why timing matters in RPS
- Links to the "is rock paper scissors fair" angle
- 1-2 short paragraphs, 100 words max

### H2: Can You Play Rock Paper Scissors on Your Phone?
*(Addresses PAA — targets mobile searchers)*
- Yes, browser-based, no app required
- Works on iOS and Android

### H2: Rock Paper Scissors on Zoom, FaceTime, or Discord
*(Addresses "how to play on video call" queries)*
- Play on our site while on a video call
- Simultaneous reveal removes the need to count to 3 together

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need to create an account?**
A: No. The game is completely anonymous — no email, no password, no profile.

**Q: How do I invite my friend?**
A: Share the unique link. When they open it, you're connected.

**Q: Is it free?**
A: Yes, always free to play.

**Q: How many rounds does a game last?**
A: Best of 3. The first player to win 2 rounds wins the match.

**Q: What if I lose my connection?**
A: The game room persists briefly. If you both reconnect to the same link, the game resumes.

## Angle (Brand Voice)

Lead with the use-case moment, not the game description. The reader is not "looking for a game" — they are trying to solve a specific problem: playing RPS with a specific person who isn't nearby.

Frame it as: **"The fastest way to challenge someone to rock paper scissors online — share one link, play in 60 seconds, winner decided fair and square."**

Do NOT start with "Rock Paper Scissors is a classic game..." That angle is taken by everyone and adds no value.
