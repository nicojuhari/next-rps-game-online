**Opening (2 sentences):** Rock paper scissors is one of the most universally known games in the world - but the official rules and counting format vary by region. Here's the complete guide, from basic rules to the best-of-3 structure.

### H2: The Basic Rules of Rock Paper Scissors

_(Snippet-optimized - short, direct, extractable)_

**Three hand gestures:**

- **Rock** - closed fist
- **Paper** - flat open hand, palm facing down
- **Scissors** - index and middle fingers extended in a V

**What beats what:**

- Rock beats Scissors (rock crushes scissors)
- Scissors beats Paper (scissors cut paper)
- Paper beats Rock (paper covers rock)

**Objective:** Both players choose simultaneously, reveal at the same time. The player whose choice beats the other's wins the round.

~150 words

### H2: What Happens in a Draw (Tie)?

- If both players show the same gesture, it's a tie
- Play again immediately until one player wins
- No move has an advantage in a tie - replay is the only rule
- ~80 words

### H2: How to Count in Rock Paper Scissors

_(Addresses the "how do you count" PAA)_

**In-person (standard):**

- "Rock, Paper, Scissors, Shoot!" - reveal on "Shoot"
- Some regions use "1, 2, 3, go" or "Jan, Ken, Pon" (Japanese origin)

**Online (real-time):**

- Both players select simultaneously on screen before either sees the result
- Eliminates lag and "peeking" problems common in video call play
- ~120 words

### H2: The Best-of-3 Format (Recommended)

_(This targets "best of 3 rock paper scissors" long-tail)_

- A full match is best of 3 rounds
- First player to win 2 rounds wins the match
- This reduces luck: a single unlucky throw doesn't end the game
- Total time: 30-60 seconds for a full best-of-3 match
- **Why best of 3?** One round is a coin flip. Three rounds reward pattern reading and consistency.
- ~150 words

### H2: Official Rules According to the WRPSA

- The World Rock Paper Scissors Association (WRPSA) standardizes competitive play
- Players use a 3-count "priming" motion, reveal on the 3rd pump
- Each player makes exactly one gesture per round - no changing after the count
- Rock = full fist; Paper = flat palm; Scissors = two-finger V
- ~100 words (with citation to WRPSA)

### H2: Variations of Rock Paper Scissors

_(Short - links to deeper content later)_

- **Rock Paper Scissors Lizard Spock:** Adds 2 more gestures; popularized by The Big Bang Theory
- **Tournament format:** Bracket play with multiple players
- **Best of 5 / Best of 7:** For longer matches when a dispute requires more rounds
- ~100 words

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is there an official version of rock paper scissors?**
A: Yes. The World Rock Paper Scissors Association (WRPSA) publishes official rules and runs international tournaments.

**Q: What if both players show the same gesture?**
A: It's a tie. Play the round again immediately.

**Q: How many rounds should you play?**
A: Best of 3 is standard. First player to win 2 rounds wins the match.

**Q: Can you play rock paper scissors online?**
A: Yes - browser-based games let two players pick simultaneously with real-time simultaneous reveal, which is fairer than video-call counting.

**Q: What's rock paper scissors called in other countries?**
A: "Jan-Ken-Pon" in Japan, "Roshambo" in parts of the US, "Schnick Schnack Schnuck" in Germany, "Pierre Feuille Ciseaux" in France.
