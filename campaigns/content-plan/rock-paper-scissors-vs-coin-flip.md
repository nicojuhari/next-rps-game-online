### H1: Rock Paper Scissors vs Coin Flip: Which Is the Fairer Way to Decide?

**Opening (3 sentences):** You need to settle something. Two options: flip a coin or play rock paper scissors. Here's what the math, psychology, and game theory say about which is actually fairer — and more fun.

### H2: The Case for Coin Flip
*(Give it a fair hearing — don't strawman)*
- True 50/50 probability
- No skill gap — neither party has an advantage
- Fast: one flip, done
- Works completely offline
- ~150 words

### H2: The Case for Rock Paper Scissors
*(Our angle — make it compelling)*
- Also 50/50 in theory (against a random opponent)
- But more engaging: both parties feel agency
- Best of 3 structure adds drama and reduces luck variance
- Simultaneous reveal removes "called it in my head" disputes
- Feels more competitive, more satisfying
- ~200 words

### H2: The Fairness Comparison — Head to Head

| Factor | Coin Flip | Rock Paper Scissors |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|
| Base probability | 50/50 | 50/50 |
| Player agency | None | High |
| Variance per decision | Single flip | Best of 3 (lower variance) |
| Perceived fairness | High | High |
| Cheating risk (in person) | Low | Low (simultaneous reveal) |
| Cheating risk (remote/text) | Low | High (without simultaneous reveal) |
| Cheating risk (online, real-time) | N/A | None — both reveal simultaneously |
| Fun factor | Low | High |
| Time to complete | 5 seconds | 30-60 seconds |
| Replay value | Low | High |

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is rock paper scissors truly 50/50?**
A: Against a fully random opponent, yes. Against a human, behavior is predictable — so there's a small skill component. This doesn't make it less fair; it makes it more interesting.

**Q: Can you cheat at online rock paper scissors?**
A: Not with a proper simultaneous-reveal system. Both players pick before either sees the other's choice.

**Q: Is a coin flip more random than rock paper scissors?**
A: A physical coin flip is closer to true random. But rock paper scissors with a best-of-3 format actually reduces variance more than a single coin flip.

**Q: What if both players know rock paper scissors strategy?**
A: Then it's a skill game, not a luck game — which makes it an even more satisfying way to settle disputes.
