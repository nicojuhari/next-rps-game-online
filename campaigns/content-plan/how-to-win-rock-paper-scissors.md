## Key Research Facts to Include

*(Sourced from SERP research — cite these)*

1. **Rock is thrown most often:** Studies show rock is chosen ~35% of the time (above the expected 33.3%)
2. **Paper is least thrown:** Only 29.6% in some studies — statistically advantageous to play paper
3. **Win-Stay, Lose-Shift:** Winners tend to repeat their move; losers tend to switch to the next in the cycle (Rock → Paper → Scissors)
4. **Beginners throw rock:** "Rock is for rookies" — male beginners especially open with rock
5. **Double-throw signal:** Players rarely throw the same move 3 times in a row — useful predictor
6. **Zhejiang University study (2014):** 360 students, 300 rounds each — confirmed Win-Stay behavior as a dominant pattern
7. **Experienced players go scissors first:** Knowing rookies go rock (and smart players expect paper), scissors is the safe experienced opener

### H2: The Win-Stay, Lose-Shift Pattern
- Winners repeat the move that won
- Losers shift to the "next" move clockwise: Rock → Paper → Scissors → Rock
- How to use this: after your opponent loses, predict their shift
- Practical example: "They just lost with Rock. They'll probably play Paper next. Play Scissors."
- ~300 words

### H2: The Beginner's Tell — Why Rock Is for Rookies
- Beginners (especially men) open with Rock
- "Rock is associated with strength" — psychological anchoring
- Counter: Play Paper in round 1 against unfamiliar opponents
- ~200 words

### H2: The Double-Throw Signal
- Players rarely play the same move 3 times in a row
- If opponent has played Scissors twice — they're unlikely to throw Scissors again
- Use this in rounds 2 and 3 to narrow predictions
- ~150 words

### H2: Reading Physical Tells (In-Person Only)
- Tight fist forming = likely Rock
- Loose relaxed hand = likely Paper
- Two fingers extended early = likely Scissors

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is paper the best move in rock paper scissors?**
A: Statistically, yes — paper beats rock, which is the most commonly thrown move. But against experienced players who know this, scissors becomes safer.

**Q: What is win-stay lose-shift?**
A: A behavioral pattern in which winners repeat the move that just won, while losers shift to the next move in the cycle (Rock → Paper → Scissors).

**Q: Does psychology really affect RPS?**
A: Yes. Multiple peer-reviewed studies show humans deviate significantly from random play, creating predictable patterns that skilled players exploit.

**Q: Can you win at rock paper scissors every time?**
A: Not every time, but consistently more than 33% against human opponents by reading patterns.
