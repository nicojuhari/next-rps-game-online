## Recommended Outline

### H1: Rock Paper Scissors as a Virtual Meeting Icebreaker: A 2-Minute Guide for Remote Teams

**Opening hook:** Name the problem - virtual meeting icebreakers are awkward. Most take 10 minutes to set up. Most require everyone to download something or create an account. Here's one that everyone already knows, takes 2 minutes, and requires zero setup.

### H2: Why Rock Paper Scissors Is the Perfect Virtual Icebreaker

- Everyone already knows the rules - zero explanation needed
- Competitive but not threatening - low stakes, high fun
- Works in groups of any size (tournament bracket format)
- Simultaneous reveal adds drama without needing a host
- Takes 2-5 minutes for a round-robin or bracket
- No download, no account, no app
- ~200 words

### H2: How to Run a Rock Paper Scissors Tournament on Your Next Call

#### H3: Option A - 1-on-1 Challenge (Pairs)

_(For small teams or quick check-ins)_

- Pair people up in the chat
- Each pair goes to the 2-player game, shares a link, plays best of 3
- Reports back with winner
- Total time: 2-3 minutes
- ~150 words

#### H3: Option B - Elimination Tournament (4-8 people)

_(For larger teams or team events)_

- Bracket format: 4 or 8 players
- Round 1: All pairs play simultaneously
- Winners advance, tournament completes in 3-4 rounds
- Use a bracket (write it in chat or shared doc)
- Total time: 5-7 minutes
- ~200 words

#### H3: Option C - Champion Challenge (Ongoing fun)

_(For recurring team meetings)_

- Designate a "reigning champion" from last meeting
- New challenger each meeting gets one best-of-3 match
- Running leaderboard keeps it going across weeks
- ~150 words

### H2: How to Play Rock Paper Scissors Online During a Zoom Call (Step by Step)

1. Keep the video call open
2. One player opens the 2-player game and shares the unique link in the chat
3. Both players open the link on their devices
4. Pick your move - both players reveal simultaneously (no cheating possible)
5. Winner wins, loser concedes dramatically on camera (this is the fun part)

_Note: No account required. Works on any device. The whole setup takes under 30 seconds._
~150 words

### H2: Why Simultaneous Reveal Makes Online RPS Fair

_(Short - addresses the "how is this different from texting?" objection)_

- In video calls, people try to play RPS by counting "1-2-3" on camera - but the lag means one person always sees the other's choice
- With online real-time RPS, both picks are locked in before either player sees the result
- ~100 words

### H2: Variations to Try With Your Team

- **Rock Paper Scissors Lizard Spock** - more moves, more outcomes, longer games
- **Dare edition** - loser of each round shares a fun fact or answers a question
- **Decision maker** - use RPS to decide who presents first, who picks the lunch place, etc.
- ~150 words

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does everyone need to create an account?**
A: No. No account needed for any player. Just open the link and play.

**Q: Does it work on phones and tablets?**
A: Yes - the game is browser-based and works on any device.

**Q: How many people can play at once?**
A: The game supports 2 players per room. For larger groups, run multiple simultaneous matches.

**Q: Can we play while on a video call?**
A: Yes. Open the game link in one browser tab and keep your video call open in another.

**Q: Is this appropriate for a work setting?**
A: Completely. It's a classic game with no mature content and zero technical requirements.
