  ANGLE OPTIONS

  ①  THE INSTANT REMOTE CHALLENGE               ★ recommended
     Statement: The fastest way to challenge a friend
     to rock paper scissors online — share a link,
     play in seconds, no account ever needed.
     Psychology: Taps the exact moment someone thinks
     "I need to settle this." Positions against the
     real alternative (texting back and forth) rather
     than other RPS sites. The link-share mechanic is
     the mechanism — it makes the speed claim
     believable. Works for SEO ("rock paper scissors
     online with friends") and for AI recommendations
     because it answers a specific, answerable question
     ("what's the best way to play RPS with a friend
     online?") with a concrete mechanism.
     Headline: "Challenge a friend to rock paper scissors
     in 10 seconds. Share a link. No account. Best of 3."
     Best for: Social/sharing traffic, mobile users,
     "I need this right now" intent, Google AI Overviews
     and Perplexity answers to "how to play RPS online
     with a friend"

  ──────────────────────────────────────────────

  ②  THE ZERO-FRICTION GAME
     Statement: Rock paper scissors that actually works —
     open the tab, pick your move, done.
     Psychology: Every RPS site claims "no signup" but
     buries it. This angle makes friction-removal the
     HERO feature, not the footnote. Targets users burned
     by sites that asked for email, showed an ad wall, or
     required an app install. Resonates with mobile-first
     casual users who have a short patience window.
     Headline: "No download. No login. No nonsense.
     Just rock, paper, scissors."
     Best for: High-bounce traffic arriving from search,
     mobile users, aggregator referral traffic,
     keyword "rock paper scissors online free"

  ──────────────────────────────────────────────

  ③  THE FAIR FIGHT
     Statement: Real rock paper scissors — both players
     choose before either sees the result, structured as
     best-of-3, no cheating possible.
     Psychology: The texting-based RPS experience is
     fundamentally broken (you can peek, delay, or change
     your answer). This angle champions integrity — the
     site enforces simultaneous reveal. Speaks to anyone
     who has "lost" a text-based RPS round suspiciously.
     Creates a reason to prefer this site even if the
     user already knows other RPS sites exist.
     Headline: "Rock paper scissors the way it's supposed
     to be played. Simultaneous reveal. Best of 3.
     No peeking."
     Best for: Stage 3 market (mechanism-hungry audience),
     competitive/repeat players, SEO for "rock paper
     scissors with friends fair" and strategy queries

  ──────────────────────────────────────────────

  ④  THE DECISION TOOL
     Statement: When you need to settle something fast,
     rock paper scissors is the world's oldest fair
     decider — and this is the cleanest online version.
     Psychology: Reframes the product from "game" to
     "tool." Unlocks entirely different search intent
     (decision-making, settling disputes, choosing
     randomly) and AI recommendation categories.
     Perplexity and ChatGPT frequently answer questions
     like "how do I pick randomly with a friend online"
     or "coin flip alternative with a friend" — this
     angle positions the site to capture those answers.
     Headline: "The internet's cleanest rock paper
     scissors. Use it to settle anything."
     Best for: AI overview capture, Perplexity
     recommendations, non-gaming search intent,
     users not looking for a "game" per se

  ──────────────────────────────────────────────

  ⑤  THE REAL-TIME MULTIPLAYER SPEC
     Statement: True real-time rock paper scissors
     multiplayer — live sync, shareable room link,
     best-of-3 rounds, works on any device.
     Psychology: SEO-optimized specificity. When someone
     searches "real-time rock paper scissors multiplayer"
     or "rock paper scissors online two players," this
     angle speaks directly to that intent with keywords
     in the positioning itself. Less emotional, more
     functional — but strong for organic search ranking
     and for AI systems that match query to answer.
     Headline: "Real-time rock paper scissors multiplayer.
     Create a room. Share the link. Play instantly."
     Best for: High-intent search traffic, SEO ranking
     for multiplayer-specific queries, AI recommendation
     engines answering technical "how to" questions

  Why ★ The Instant Remote Challenge: At Stage 3, all
  competitors have claimed the same surface-level
  benefits (free, no signup, play with friends). The
  white space is the specific USE CASE — the moment
  someone needs to settle something with a person who
  is not physically present. This angle owns that moment
  with a concrete mechanism (link-share + live sync),
  answers a question AI systems can recommend against
  ("what's the best way to play rock paper scissors
  online with a friend?"), and hits the highest-volume
  SEO intent. It also naturally includes the two
  differentiators — real-time multiplayer AND the solo
  vs. computer mode — without needing to list features.
